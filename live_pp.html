Privacy Policy

HabiVox: Habit & Money Tracker

This privacy policy explains what information HabiVox collects, how it is used, and how we protect your data. Our goal is to keep your experience safe, secure, and transparent.

Information We Collect

HabiVox collects the minimum information required to operate your account and deliver personalized habit and expense tracking features.

  • Google login information: We use Firebase Auth for Google sign-in. This enables secure account access and does not store your Google password.
  • Habit data: Personal habit entries, progress, reminders, and completion status.
  • Expense data: Personal expense records, categories, amounts, and related notes.
  • Notification preferences: Push notification settings used only for reminders and alerts.

How We Store Your Data

All user data is securely stored in Google Firestore. Firestore is a managed cloud database service under Google’s privacy and security practices.

We do not store payment card data or highly sensitive identification information. Your tracked habits and expenses remain within your HabiVox account.

Authentication

HabiVox uses Firebase Authentication to sign users in with Google. The service handles authentication securely, and HabiVox receives only the information needed to establish your account.

Notifications

To help you stay on track, HabiVox may send push notifications for reminders and habit alerts. Notifications are only used for app-related alerts and are not shared with third parties.

Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell your data to third parties. Your information is used solely to provide the HabiVox service.

Third-party services used for authentication and data storage include Firebase Auth and Google Firestore. These services process data under their own privacy policies.

Security

We follow industry-standard practices to protect your data. Access to your data is limited to what is necessary for the app to function.

Because no system is completely impervious to risk, we encourage users to protect their account credentials and use strong passwords for associated Google accounts.
